Planetary Facts

Neptune is the windiest planet in the Solar System, with winds reaching speeds of over 2,000 km/h.

Atmospheric Composition

Neptune's atmosphere is made up of hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives it its distinctive blue color.

Moons of Neptune

Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest. Triton orbits Neptune in the opposite direction to the planet's rotation (retrograde orbit).

Geological Features

Neptune is an ice giant with a rocky core surrounded by water, ammonia, and methane ices. It has no solid surface.

Exploration Missions

Neptune was visited by Voyager 2 in 1989, which provided the only close-up images of the planet and its moons.
