Planetary Facts

Uranus is unique in that it rotates on its side, making it different from all other planets in the Solar System.

Atmospheric Composition

The atmosphere of Uranus is mostly hydrogen (83%) and helium (15%), with trace amounts of methane, which gives the planet its blue-green color.

Moons of Uranus

Uranus has 27 known moons, with Titania and Oberon being the largest. Most of the moons are named after characters from Shakespearean literature.

Geological Features

Uranus is an ice giant, composed mostly of icy materials such as water, ammonia, and methane, with no solid surface.

Exploration Missions

Uranus was visited by Voyager 2 in 1986, which provided the only close-up images of the planet and its moons.
