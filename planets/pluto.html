Planetary Facts

Pluto was reclassified from a planet to a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union.

Atmospheric Composition

Pluto's atmosphere consists mainly of nitrogen, with traces of methane and carbon monoxide.

Moons of Pluto

Pluto's largest moon, Charon, is nearly half the size of Pluto, forming a unique binary system.

Geological Features

Pluto has mountains made of water ice and vast plains of frozen nitrogen. Its surface is a mix of rock and ice.

Exploration Missions

Pluto was visited by NASA's New Horizons spacecraft in 2015, providing the first detailed images of the dwarf planet.
