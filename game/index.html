Solar Trek NEO (Near-Earth-object)

What are NEOs?

NEOs are asteroids and comets that come close to Earth's orbit. They are essential to study as they could potentially collide with Earth, and they also offer insights into the early solar system.

Types of NEOs

NEOs are divided into Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs) and Near-Earth Comets (NECs). NEAs make up most of the known NEO population.

Why Do We Track Them?

Scientists monitor NEOs to assess the risk of impact and to learn about their composition, which can help us prepare for potential future asteroid mining or deflection missions.
